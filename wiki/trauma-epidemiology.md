# Trauma Epidemiology: The Global and US Burden of Injury

**Summary**: Statistics on the incidence, mortality, and economic cost of traumatic injury in the United States and worldwide, with breakdowns by cause and age group.

**Sources**: PHTLS Prehospital Trauma Life Support 10th Edition.pdf

**Last updated**: 2026-04-14

---

## Key Statistics — United States

- Unintentional injury is the **4th overall leading cause of death** among all ages annually in the US
- Almost half of US injury deaths result from: **motor vehicle crashes, falls, or firearms**
- Economic impact: approximately **$1.1 trillion** in 2019 (fatal + nonfatal, National Safety Council)
- Identifying hemorrhage and rapidly transporting to a trauma center saves approximately **$1.2 million per patient** in lifetime wage and productivity losses

### Leading Causes by Age Group (2019, CDC/WISQARS)

| Age Group | #1 Cause of Unintentional Injury Death |
|-----------|----------------------------------------|
| 1–4 years | Drowning |
| 5–24 years | Motor vehicle crashes (MVC) |
| 25–64 years | Poisoning (opioid epidemic) |
| 65+ years | Falls |

Motor vehicle crashes persist as a major cause across virtually all age groups. Poisoning (driven by the opioid epidemic) has become the leading cause in the 25–64 age bracket and is expected to continue rising.

### Global Inequality in Trauma Burden

- Low- and middle-income countries (LMICs) own only **half** of the world's vehicles but account for **90%** of all road traffic deaths
- **93%** of the world's road traffic deaths occur in LMICs, which account for only 60% of the world's vehicles
- Falls: 650,000 deaths/year worldwide; disproportionately in LMICs

### Projected Trends to 2030

- Falls and motor vehicle crashes are the **only two traumatic causes predicted to increase** worldwide by 2030
- WHO predicts road traffic accidents will rise to the **7th leading cause of death globally** by 2030 without intervention
- The **Second Decade of Action for Road Traffic Safety (2021–2030)** established by the UN aims for a 50% reduction in worldwide road traffic deaths

## Why These Numbers Matter for Prehospital Care

Trauma is not random. Most traumatic deaths fit the definition of a **preventable event** caused by carelessness or poor infrastructure, not a chance event. This makes prevention possible and makes the prehospital care practitioner's work part of a continuum that includes:

1. Injury prevention (pre-event)
2. Response and field care (event)
3. Definitive hospital care (post-event)

See [[phases-of-trauma-care]] for the framework. See [[phtls-chapter-1-history]] for the full discussion of trauma as a public health problem.

## US Trauma-Related Costs (Snapshot)

- 3 million older Americans treated in EDs for fall-related injuries each year
- Over 800,000 hospitalized from falls annually
- Estimated total medical cost of fall injuries exceeded **$50 billion** in 2015
