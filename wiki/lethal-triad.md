# Lethal Triad

**Summary**: The lethal triad of hypothermia, coagulopathy, and metabolic acidosis are markers of severe anaerobic metabolism and impending death from hemorrhagic shock; each component worsens the others in a self-reinforcing cycle.

**Sources**: PHTLS Prehospital Trauma Life Support 10th Edition.pdf

**Last updated**: 2026-04-14

---

## Overview

The lethal triad describes three interrelated physiologic derangements seen in severely injured, poorly resuscitated trauma patients. They are not causes of death in isolation, but **markers of advanced anaerobic metabolism** that signal the interventions required to prevent death. (source: PHTLS Prehospital Trauma Life Support 10th Edition.pdf)

The three components are mutually reinforcing — each worsens the others:

```
Hemorrhage
    ↓
Hypothermia ──────────────────────────────┐
(impairs clotting enzymes)                │
    ↓                                     │
Coagulopathy ────────────────────────────►│ More hemorrhage
(clotting factors lost/dysfunctional)     │
    ↓                                     │
Metabolic Acidosis ──────────────────────►│
(H+ accumulation from anaerobic           │
metabolism impairs clotting + myocardium) │
    └─────────────────────────────────────┘
```

## Component 1: Hypothermia

**Definition**: Core body temperature below normal (< 36°C / 96.8°F in the trauma context)

**Causes in trauma**:
- Exposure to cold environment
- Loss of energy production from anaerobic metabolism (body can no longer generate heat)
- Administration of room-temperature or cold IV fluids
- Wet clothing increasing heat loss by convection

**Effects**:
- Directly impairs blood-clotting enzyme function (enzymes require near-normal temperature)
- Worsens coagulopathy, myocardial dysfunction, and vasoconstriction
- Increases hyperkalemia
- Even a **few degrees** of temperature drop impairs clotting significantly

**Prevention/Treatment**:
- Remove wet clothing (including blood-soaked)
- Warm patient compartment to ≥ 85°F (29°C)
- Cover with warm blankets or plastic sheeting
- Administer IV fluids warmed to 102°F (39°C)
- Heated, humidified oxygen in intubated patients

## Component 2: Coagulopathy

**Definition**: Impairment of normal blood clotting capability

**Causes in trauma**:
- **Dilution**: large volume crystalloid resuscitation dilutes clotting factors
- **Consumption**: clotting factors are used up at injury sites (consumptive coagulopathy)
- **Hypothermia**: clotting enzymes dysfunctional at low temperatures
- **Acidosis**: pH < 7.20 impairs clotting cascade enzymes

**Effects**: Continued or worsened hemorrhage despite hemostatic attempts

**Treatment**:
- Minimize crystalloid; prioritize blood products (PRBCs + FFP at 1:1 ratio)
- Cryoprecipitate for fibrinogen replacement
- [[tranexamic-acid]] (TXA) to prevent fibrinolysis
- Platelets as needed
- Control hemorrhage surgically at definitive care

## Component 3: Metabolic Acidosis

**Definition**: pH decrease (excess H⁺) caused by lactic acid accumulation from anaerobic metabolism

**Causes in trauma**:
- Cellular hypoperfusion → anaerobic metabolism → lactate production
- Worsened by large-volume normal saline (hyperchloremic acidosis)

**Effects**:
- pH < 7.20: severe myocardial depression
- Impairs clotting cascade enzymes
- Vasodilation at severe levels (paradoxically worsening hypotension)
- Shifts oxyhemoglobin dissociation curve rightward (increases O₂ off-loading — a compensatory response)

**Treatment**:
- Restore perfusion (hemorrhage control + oxygenation) → cells return to aerobic metabolism → lactate cleared
- Lactated Ringer's or balanced crystalloids preferred over normal saline to avoid hyperchloremic acidosis

## Connection to Damage Control Resuscitation

Recognition of the lethal triad drove the shift in resuscitation philosophy:
- **Old approach**: large-volume crystalloid resuscitation to normalize BP
- **New approach** (Damage Control Resuscitation): permissive hypotension + 1:1 blood products + early hemorrhage control + hypothermia prevention

This strategy has significantly reduced ARDS and multiple organ failure in the immediate trauma period.
