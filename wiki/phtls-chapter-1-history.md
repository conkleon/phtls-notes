## Key Figures

| Person | Contribution |
|--------|-------------|
| Baron Dominique Jean Larrey | Napoleon's chief physician; created "flying ambulances" 1797; established principles of prehospital care still used today |
| J. D. "Deke" Farrington, MD | Father of EMS in the US; "Death in a Ditch" (1967); drove EMT training development |
| R. Adams Cowley, MD | Founder of MIEMSS (one of first US trauma centers); coined the "Golden Hour" concept |

## PHTLS Program Milestones

- **1978**: ATLS prototype field-tested in Auburn, Nebraska (origin of the trauma life support movement)
- **1970**: National Registry of EMTs (NREMT) established
- **1973**: Federal EMS legislation — defined 15 components of an integrated EMS system
- **Early 1990s**: PHTLS launched internationally (UK, Mexico first)
- **2010**: NAEMT began offering TCCC Course
- **2019**: 25,600+ international practitioners received PHTLS education; taught in 70+ countries

---

## Epidemiology Highlights

- Unintentional injury: 4th overall leading cause of death in the US
- 1.3 million road traffic deaths/year worldwide (WHO)
- 4.4 million injury deaths/year globally
- Economic impact of trauma in the US: ~$1.1 trillion (2019, National Safety Council)
- Properly identified and transported hemorrhage patients save ~$1.2M per patient in lifetime productivity

See [[trauma-epidemiology]] for full detail.

---

## What Changed Since Previous Editions (Notable in 10th Edition)

- Emphasis on blood and plasma over crystalloid for prehospital resuscitation
- Damage Control Resuscitation (1:1 blood product ratio) as the standard
- Integration of TCCC principles throughout
- Expanded international reach and regional education committees (Europe 2018, Latin America 2019, Middle East 2021)
